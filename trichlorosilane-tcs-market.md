<p><p>Trichlorosilane (TCS) is a key chemical used in the production of high-purity silicon for the semiconductor and solar industries. There are two primary market processes for producing TCS: the Direct Chlorination (DC) process and the Hydrochlorination (HC) process. The DC process involves the direct reaction of silicon with chlorine, providing a straightforward synthesis route. In contrast, the HC process combines silicon with hydrogen chloride, offering an alternative method that may be more efficient in certain applications. Both processes cater to the growing demand for silicon-based products.</p></p>
