<p><p>The roofing underlying materials market includes three main types: Asphalt-Saturated Felt, Modified Asphalt, and Non-Bitumen Synthetic. Asphalt-Saturated Felt is a traditional, cost-effective option made from organic or fiberglass base saturated with asphalt, offering basic moisture protection. Modified Asphalt incorporates polymers for enhanced durability, flexibility, and resistance to extreme weather. Non-Bitumen Synthetic materials, made from polymers like PVC or TPO, provide lightweight, eco-friendly, and highly durable alternatives with superior resistance to UV rays and chemicals. Each type caters to varying roofing needs and budgets.</p></p>
