<p><p>Fuel-grade petcoke is categorized mainly into two types: shot coke and sponge coke. Shot coke appears as hard, dense particles and is typically produced in a delayed coking process, making it suitable for specific applications in energy production due to its high carbon content. Sponge coke, on the other hand, is more porous and lighter, characterized by its sponge-like structure. It is often used in the aluminum and steel industries, where its properties provide advantages in certain manufacturing processes.</p></p>
